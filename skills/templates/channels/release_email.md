# Release Email

Write a release announcement email that tells people what shipped, why it matters, and how to use it.

## Format

- 200-400 words
- Plain text feel — not HTML newsletter energy
- Subject line as the headline

## Structure

- Subject line that gets opened. Clear value, not clickbait. "[Product] now supports X" beats "Exciting news from the team!"
- Lead with the benefit, not the feature. "You can now deploy in half the time" before "We added parallel build support."
- What shipped — be specific. Version numbers, feature names, config changes.
- Why it matters — one or two sentences connecting the feature to what users actually care about.
- How to use it — a code snippet, a link to docs, or a quick "just update to v2.4 and enable X in your config."
- One clear CTA. Try it. Read the docs. Upgrade. Pick one.

## Style

- Written by a person, not a department. "We shipped" not "The team is pleased to announce."
- Conversational but respectful of people's inboxes — every sentence should earn its place
- Specific over general. Numbers, names, concrete changes.
- It's okay to be brief. A 150-word email that says everything is better than 400 words of padding.

## What to avoid

- "We're excited to announce" (you're always excited, it means nothing)
- Multiple CTAs competing for attention
- Burying the actual change under three paragraphs of context
- Marketing superlatives (revolutionary, game-changing, industry-leading)
