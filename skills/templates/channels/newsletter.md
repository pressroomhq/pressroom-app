# Newsletter

Write a company newsletter update — a quick, friendly roundup of what happened this week.

## Format

- 300-500 words
- "This week in [company]" format
- Markdown with H3 subheadings for each section
- Links to docs, blog posts, or repos where relevant

## Structure

- Open with the most interesting thing that happened — the thing a reader would actually tell a coworker about
- What shipped — new features, releases, fixes worth mentioning
- What's coming — a brief look-ahead, no promises you can't keep
- One community highlight — a cool use case, a contributor shoutout, an interesting support ticket, a pull request worth noting
- Close with a one-liner — a thought, a thank you, or a pointer to where people can get involved

## Style

- Casual and informative, like an update from a colleague, not a press release
- First person plural ("we") feels natural here
- Keep each section tight — 2-3 sentences per topic. This is a scan, not a read.
- It's okay to have personality — a joke, an aside, a self-deprecating note about a bug you shipped

## What to avoid

- Salesy language — this goes to people who already use/follow your product
- Empty sections. If nothing shipped this week, skip that section rather than padding it.
- Walls of text. If a topic needs more than a paragraph, link to a blog post.
- "Don't forget to follow us on social media" — your newsletter readers are already engaged
