# LinkedIn Post

Write a LinkedIn post that reads like a person sharing a genuine insight, not a brand pushing content.

## Format

- 150-300 words
- Plain text, no markdown formatting (LinkedIn doesn't render it)
- Line breaks between paragraphs for readability

## Structure

- Hook in the first line. Pattern interrupt, bold claim, or question. This is the only line visible before "see more" — it must earn the click.
- Don't start with "I" — start with the insight.
- Body develops one clear idea. Not three. Not five. One.
- End with a thought-provoking question or a takeaway the reader walks away with. Not a CTA. Not "drop a comment below."

## Style

- Write like a human sharing something they learned, not a marketer distributing content
- Conversational but not sloppy — this is a professional network
- Specific beats general. "We cut deploy time from 12 minutes to 90 seconds" beats "We improved our deployment process"
- Show your thinking, not just your conclusions

## What to avoid

- Hashtags unless they're genuinely useful (max 3, at the end)
- Bullet-point listicles unless the content genuinely demands it
- "Excited to announce" or "Thrilled to share" openers
- Emoji-heavy formatting (one or two is fine, a wall of them is not)
- Tagging people for engagement bait
