# Email Sequence

Design email automation sequences — welcome series, nurture campaigns, re-engagement flows, and lifecycle emails.

## Format

- Each email: 100-250 words (respect the inbox)
- Subject lines: specific, benefit-driven, no ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation
- Preview text: 40-90 characters, complements (not repeats) the subject
- Clear single CTA per email

## Sequence Types

### Welcome Sequence (3-5 emails)
- **Email 1** (immediate): Deliver what they signed up for. Set expectations for what's coming.
- **Email 2** (Day 1-2): Quick win — one actionable thing they can do right now
- **Email 3** (Day 3-4): Social proof or case study — show what's possible
- **Email 4** (Day 5-7): Core value prop deep dive — the thing that makes you different
- **Email 5** (Day 7-10): Soft CTA — invite to next step (demo, trial, upgrade)

### Nurture Sequence (5-8 emails)
- Space 2-4 days apart
- Each email teaches one useful thing — no pure pitch emails
- Alternate between educational content and light product mentions
- Build toward a conversion CTA in the final 2 emails

### Re-engagement (3 emails)
- **Email 1:** "We noticed you've been quiet" — share what's new, one compelling update
- **Email 2:** Best content or feature they missed — make it worth re-engaging
- **Email 3:** Breakup — "should we stop emailing?" with clear unsubscribe

## Structure Per Email

1. **Subject line** — specific, curiosity-driven or benefit-driven
2. **Opening line** — hook or context (never "Dear valued customer")
3. **Body** — one idea, developed clearly
4. **CTA** — one action, low friction, obvious next step
5. **Sign-off** — from a real person, not "The Team"

## Style

- One email, one job — never combine a product update with a sales pitch
- Value before ask — earn the right to pitch by being useful first
- Write from a person, not a brand (use first person, have a voice)
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max), generous whitespace

## What to avoid

- "No-reply" sender addresses
- Misleading subject lines ("RE:" tricks, fake urgency)
- Sending more than one email per day in any sequence
- Generic content that could come from any company
- Burying the CTA or having competing CTAs
