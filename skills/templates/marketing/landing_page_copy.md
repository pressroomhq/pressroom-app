# Landing Page Copy

Write conversion-focused copy for landing pages, homepages, feature pages, and pricing pages.

## Format

- Section-based output with clear H2 headers
- Each section: headline + 1-3 short paragraphs or bullet points
- CTAs written out with button text suggestions
- Include placeholder notes for social proof elements: [TESTIMONIAL], [LOGO BAR], [STAT]

## Page Structure

### Above the Fold
- **Headline:** What you do + who it's for, in 10 words or less. Lead with the outcome, not the product.
- **Subheadline:** One sentence expanding the headline — add specificity or proof
- **CTA:** Action verb + what they get. "Start your free trial" not "Learn more"
- **Supporting element note:** [HERO IMAGE/DEMO GIF/VIDEO suggestion]

### Problem Section
- Name the pain the reader already feels — use their language, not yours
- 2-3 specific symptoms they'll recognize ("spending hours on X", "losing deals because Y")
- Don't exaggerate — if you nail the real problem, it resonates without drama

### Solution Section
- How you solve it — benefits first, features second
- Each benefit: what it does → why it matters → proof if available
- 3-5 key benefits max — don't list everything, pick the ones that win deals

### Social Proof
- [TESTIMONIAL: specific result quote from named customer with role/company]
- [LOGO BAR: 4-6 recognizable customer logos]
- [STAT: specific number — "10,000+ teams" or "cut setup time by 80%"]

### How It Works
- 3-4 steps, dead simple
- Each step: verb-led title + one sentence explanation
- Show it's easy, not comprehensive

### Objection Handling
- Address the top 2-3 reasons people don't buy (price, complexity, switching cost)
- Be direct — "Yes, there's a learning curve. Here's how we handle it: ..."

### Final CTA
- Restate the core value prop in one line
- Same CTA as above the fold — consistency builds confidence
- Add risk reducer: "Free for 14 days. No credit card required."

## Style

- Clarity over cleverness — if the reader has to think about what you mean, rewrite it
- Specific beats generic: "Deploy in 4 minutes" beats "Quick and easy setup"
- Write in second person ("you") — talk to the reader, not about them
- Confident but not arrogant — state what you do well, don't trash competitors

## What to avoid

- "Welcome to our website" or any variant
- Feature dumps without benefit framing
- Multiple competing CTAs on the same page
- Vague claims ("best-in-class", "world-class", "cutting-edge")
- Long paragraphs — if a section is more than 3 lines, break it up
