# Cold Email

Write B2B cold outreach emails that read like a peer reaching out, not a vendor pitching.

## Format

- 50-125 words per email (ruthlessly short)
- Plain text — no HTML, no images, no formatting
- Subject line: 2-4 words, lowercase, looks internal ("quick question", "re: your api")

## Structure

- **Line 1:** Personalized observation about the recipient — something specific you noticed about their company, role, or recent work. Not flattery. Not "I love your company." Something that proves you did 30 seconds of research.
- **Line 2-3:** Connect that observation to a problem they likely have. One sentence max.
- **Line 4:** What you do about it. Proof point if you have one (specific number, named customer, concrete result). One sentence.
- **Line 5:** Low-friction CTA. Not "book a 30-minute demo." Try "worth a look?" or "want me to send a quick breakdown?"

## Follow-Up Sequence

Write 3-4 follow-ups, each taking a different angle:

1. **Email 1** (Day 0): Core pitch as above
2. **Email 2** (Day 3): New angle — share a relevant insight, case study, or data point. Not "just following up."
3. **Email 3** (Day 7): Social proof angle — who else in their space uses you, what result they got
4. **Email 4** (Day 14): Breakup email — brief, gives them an easy out ("totally understand if the timing's off")

## Style

- Write like a human who happens to work at a company, not a "sales professional"
- Every sentence must earn its place — if it doesn't add information or advance the ask, cut it
- No jargon, no buzzwords, no "leverage" or "synergize" or "solution"
- Specific beats generic: "cut onboarding from 3 weeks to 4 days" beats "improved efficiency"

## What to avoid

- "I hope this email finds you well"
- Walls of text or bullet-point feature lists
- Multiple CTAs or asks in one email
- Mentioning your company name before the recipient's problem
- HTML formatting, images, or tracking pixels (kills deliverability)
- "Just checking in" or "circling back" without new value
