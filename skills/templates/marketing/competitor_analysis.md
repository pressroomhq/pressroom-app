# Competitor Analysis

Create competitor comparison pages, alternative pages, and positioning content for SEO and sales enablement.

## Format

- Long-form (1000-2000 words) for comparison pages
- Structured with H2/H3 headers, comparison tables, and clear recommendations
- Include SEO-optimized title suggestions for each page type

## Page Types

### "[Competitor] Alternative" Page
- Target: people searching for options beyond a specific competitor
- **Title pattern:** "Best [Competitor] Alternatives in [Year]" or "[Your Product]: The [Competitor] Alternative for [Audience]"
- Lead with why people look for alternatives (common pain points with the competitor)
- Position yourself as the answer to those specific pain points
- Include 2-3 other alternatives briefly (shows objectivity, captures broader search intent)
- End with migration/switching section

### "Us vs [Competitor]" Page
- Target: people comparing you directly
- **Title pattern:** "[You] vs [Competitor]: [Key Differentiator]"
- TL;DR comparison at the top (table or bullet points)
- Section-by-section honest comparison (features, pricing, use cases, support)
- "Who should choose [Competitor]" section — yes, be honest about when they're the better fit
- "Who should choose [You]" section — your sweet spot
- End with specific proof points (customer switches, benchmarks, quotes)

### Competitor Landscape Overview
- Target: people early in research
- **Title pattern:** "Best [Category] Tools in [Year]" or "[Category] Comparison Guide"
- Brief intro on what to look for when evaluating
- Structured comparison of 5-8 players including yourself
- For each: one-paragraph summary, strengths, weaknesses, best for
- Comparison table with key decision criteria
- Your honest recommendation at the end

## Research Framework

For each competitor, cover:
1. **Product:** Core features, what they do best, known limitations
2. **Pricing:** Plans, per-seat vs usage-based, hidden costs, free tier
3. **Audience:** Who they're built for, company size, industry focus
4. **Strengths:** What users genuinely like (pull from reviews, not marketing)
5. **Weaknesses:** Real complaints from G2, Reddit, HN — not strawmen

## Style

- Honest — acknowledge competitor strengths. Readers smell bias and leave.
- Specific — "their API rate limits at 100 req/min on the free plan" not "limited API"
- Helpful — help the reader make the right choice, even if it's not you
- Updated — include the year in titles, note when info was last verified

## What to avoid

- Trashing competitors without evidence
- Claiming you're better at everything (nobody believes it)
- Ignoring competitors' genuine advantages
- Using competitor trademarks inappropriately
- Outdated pricing or feature information
