# AI SEO

Optimize content to be cited by AI search engines — Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, and Copilot.

## Context

AI search is replacing clicks. 45% of Google searches now show AI Overviews. Brands are 6.5x more likely to be cited via third-party sources than their own domains. This skill is about getting your content cited, not just ranked.

## Three Pillars

### 1. Structure (Make It Extractable)

AI models pull structured content. Give them what they want:

- **Definition blocks:** Start sections with clear, quotable definitions ("X is a Y that does Z")
- **Step-by-step formats:** Numbered instructions that AI can extract directly
- **Comparison tables:** Feature comparisons, pros/cons lists, pricing tables
- **FAQ sections:** Question-and-answer format at the bottom of key pages
- **Statistics with context:** "X increased by Y% (Source, Year)" — always include the source and date

### 2. Authority (Make It Citable)

AI models prefer authoritative sources:

- **Cite your sources:** Link to primary research, studies, official docs
- **Include original data:** Surveys, benchmarks, case studies with real numbers
- **Expert quotes:** Named experts with credentials, not anonymous testimonials
- **Freshness signals:** "Last updated: [date]" prominently displayed
- **E-E-A-T alignment:** Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness in author bios and about pages

### 3. Presence (Be Where AI Looks)

AI models pull from more than your website:

- **Wikipedia:** If eligible, ensure your company's entry is accurate and sourced
- **Reddit & forums:** Genuine participation in relevant communities (not astroturfing)
- **Review sites:** G2, Capterra, Trustpilot — maintain accurate profiles
- **YouTube:** Video content with accurate transcripts and descriptions
- **Industry publications:** Guest posts, interviews, contributed articles
- **GitHub:** Open-source presence if applicable (README quality matters)

## Content Optimization Checklist

For each piece of content, ensure:

- [ ] Clear, quotable answer to the target question in the first 2 paragraphs
- [ ] At least one structured element (table, numbered list, FAQ)
- [ ] Statistics with sources and dates
- [ ] Internal links to related content (builds topical authority)
- [ ] Schema markup where applicable (FAQ, HowTo, Article)
- [ ] "Last updated" date visible on the page
- [ ] Canonical URL set correctly

## Most-Cited Content Types

Prioritize creating these formats (by AI citation frequency):
1. **Comparison pages** (33%) — "X vs Y", "Best tools for Z"
2. **Definitive guides** (15%) — comprehensive, authoritative single-topic resources
3. **Original research** (12%) — surveys, benchmarks, data studies
4. **Best-of lists** (10%) — curated recommendations with reasoning

## Technical Requirements

- Don't block AI crawlers in robots.txt: GPTBot, PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot, Google-Extended, Bingbot
- Ensure fast page loads (AI crawlers time out on slow pages)
- Use semantic HTML (proper heading hierarchy, article tags, structured data)
- Maintain XML sitemap with lastmod dates

## What to avoid

- Thin content that restates what's already everywhere (AI won't cite commodity information)
- Keyword stuffing — AI models understand intent, not keyword density
- Gating all content behind login walls (AI can't crawl it)
- Ignoring third-party presence — your own site is only part of the picture
